Indian Institute of Technology Bombay


Hi there, I’m Reeyansh Shah, a third-year student in the Electrical Engineering Department at IIT Bombay, also pursuing a minor in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science.

My interests lie in Deep Learning and Artificial Intelligence and am passionate about quantitative finance and open source contribution.

During the summer, I interned at Texas Instruments with the Jacinto Baseport Team, where I worked on embedded Linux development for automotive-grade platforms. My primary responsibility was porting Red Hat In-Vehicle Operating System (RHIVOS) to TI’s AM69-SK and J784S4 evaluation boards. I configured the Linux kernel, U-Boot bootloader, and device trees, and integrated essential RPM packages to enable networking and the dnf package manager. I also brought up the display subsystem and validated graphical output by running containerized applications such as the Swell Foop game. Additionally, I implemented a camera-to-display pipeline using GStreamer, and demonstrated virtualization by running Alpine Linux as a guest OS inside QEMU. The internship gave me deep exposure to embedded systems, system-level Linux workflows, and real-time multimedia pipelines.

Beyond my academic and professional pursuits, I am an avid Harry Potter fan and enjoy swimming in my free time.
